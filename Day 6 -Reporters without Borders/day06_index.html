Press Freedom

How does a country's wealth and income inequality relate to how freely its journalists can work? Toggle between GDP per capita and Gini coefficient to explore two different lenses on the same question.

Sources: RSF World Press Freedom Index 2024 · World Bank GDP per capita (PPP) · World Inequality Database (Gini coefficient, pre-tax)
Note: Gini data unavailable for ~30 countries. GDP shown on logarithmic scale.
GDP lens

Wealthier countries strongly tend toward higher press freedom — but Gulf states like Qatar, UAE, and Saudi Arabia are clear outliers, combining high GDP with restricted press.

Gini lens

The relationship is messier. High inequality doesn't reliably predict low press freedom — some unequal countries score surprisingly well, making this a more nuanced story.

Both lenses

Hover any dot to see both metrics at once. The countries that break the pattern — rich but unfree, or poor but free — are often the most interesting to examine.
